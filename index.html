About Me

My journey with computers began in early childhood when I was first introduced to technology during my kindergarten years. From an early age, I developed a strong curiosity for how machines functioned, often experimenting with hardware components and system configurations.

By fifth grade, I had started learning programming through online resources and hands-on experimentation with Visual Basic and system-level customization. Through repeated trial, error, and system recovery, I learned how to diagnose, repair, and maintain computing environments independently.

This habit of learning by building, breaking, and rebuilding evolved into a strong foundation in software engineering, data science, and machine learning. Today, I actively work on predictive analytics, LLM-based systems, and scalable ML pipelines, with a focus on transforming academic research into reliable, industry-grade solutions.
